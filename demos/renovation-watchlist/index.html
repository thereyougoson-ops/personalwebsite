The Renovation Watchlist

Rent-controlled buildings are rarely emptied by mass eviction. More often they go quiet — renovated, one unit at a time. Unpermitted construction is often the earliest public signal. This is a ranked watchlist of the protected San Francisco buildings showing that pressure now.

About 70,000 SF homes are under rent control.

Most sit in buildings of two or more units, built before June 1979 — the city's largest source of unsubsidized affordable housing. Occupied, they can't be re-priced.

02

So some of the work goes unpermitted.

Work that needs a permit, done without one, is a quiet way to upgrade — and re-rent — a protected unit. The Department of Building Inspection logs each one as a Notice of Violation.

Method & caveats

How this is built

The data

  • DBI Notices of Violation — the permit-required violation signal.
  • Assessor Secured Property Tax Roll — rent-control eligibility, units, and assessed renovation value.

Snapshot refreshed —. Re-run pipeline/build_watchlist.py to update.

The signal

SF's DBI data has no "work without permit" flag. The signal lives in the free-text item field — violations such as building permit required, provide plumbing/electrical permit, and failure to file for necessary permits.

The score

Each building is scored 0–100 on four normalized parts:

  • 45% repeat violations (log-scaled count)
  • 25% recency of the latest violation
  • 20% assessed improvement value (renovation upside)
  • 10% residential units at stake

Read this carefully

A Notice of Violation is an alleged violation, not a conviction — treat it as a signal, not proof.

This is not an eviction predictor. It flags renovation pressure on protected stock, nothing more.

Rent-control eligibility is a proxy (pre-1979, 2+ units); condominium parcels and some edge cases can be imperfect.
